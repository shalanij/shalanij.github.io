About
who I am

I am a Social Technologist and researcher examining how emerging technologies, including generative AI, are reshaping teaching, learning, and assessment in higher education. I am a PhD Candidate in Information Culture and Data Stewardship at the School of Computing and Information, University of Pittsburgh, where my dissertation investigates how MLIS faculty assess student learning in the age of generative AI.

My perspective is shaped by nine years of teaching experience as a Lecturer in Library and Information Science at the University of Kelaniya, Sri Lanka, alongside my work as a first-generation international scholar navigating the intersections of technology, pedagogy, and institutional practice. I bring a pragmatist, mixed-methods orientation to questions that matter at the human–technology interface.

Before coming to Pittsburgh, I developed research interests spanning information systems, digital wellbeing, and library anxiety — threads that continue to inform how I think about the evolving roles of educators and learners in AI-mediated environments.

Research Interests

  • Learning Assessment & Academic Integrity
